Privacy Policy

Effective Date: May 1, 2026  ·  Last Updated: May 18, 2026

Summary: Case Helix is a B2B platform. Our clients are law firms. We process claimant data exclusively to provide lead scoring on their behalf. We do not sell claimant data, share it with other firms, or use it for any purpose outside of the services we provide to our clients. We take HIPAA obligations seriously and operate under Business Associate Agreements with our law firm clients.

1. Who We Are

Case Helix, LLC ("Case Helix," "we," "us," or "our") provides evidence-aware disability lead scoring services to contingency law firms and attorneys ("Clients"). Our platform analyzes incoming claimant leads against Social Security Administration eligibility criteria and returns scored results to our Clients to assist with intake prioritization.

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and protect information in connection with our services. It applies to our website at casehelix.com and to our lead scoring platform.

2. Information We Collect

Information from law firm clients (Account Data): When a law firm registers to use Case Helix, we collect:

  • Firm name, address, and contact information
  • Names and email addresses of authorized users at the firm
  • Billing and payment information (processed by our payment processor; we do not store full card numbers)
  • CRM integration credentials and configuration settings

Information we process on behalf of clients (Lead Data): To score leads, our Clients provide us with claimant information, which may include:

  • Claimant name, phone number, and email address
  • General disability type or medical condition category
  • SSA application status and stage
  • Age, work history category, and functional limitation indicators
  • Notes or intake information provided by the claimant

Website usage data: When you visit our website, we collect standard technical data including browser type, referring URL, pages visited, and IP address. We use this data to operate and improve the website.

3. How We Use Information

We use Account Data to:

  • Create and manage client accounts
  • Provide, maintain, and improve our services
  • Process payments and send billing communications
  • Communicate with clients about their accounts and our services
  • Comply with legal obligations

We use Lead Data exclusively to:

  • Score individual leads against SSA eligibility criteria on behalf of our Client
  • Return scored results and recommendations to the Client that submitted the lead
  • Improve the accuracy of our scoring models in aggregate, anonymized form

We do not use Lead Data for marketing, advertising, or any purpose unrelated to the scoring services we provide to the submitting Client.

4. How We Share Information

We do not sell claimant data. We do not share Lead Data with any third party except as described below:

  • With the submitting Client: Scored results are returned only to the law firm that submitted the lead. Lead Data is never shared with other law firms, lead brokers, or other parties.
  • Service providers: We share data with vendors who help us operate our platform (e.g., cloud hosting, payment processing, CRM integrations) under confidentiality agreements and, where required, Business Associate Agreements.
  • Legal compliance: We may disclose information when required by law, court order, or to protect our legal rights.
  • Business transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, data may be transferred to the successor entity, subject to the same privacy commitments in this Policy.

5. HIPAA and Protected Health Information

Disability law firms that use Case Helix may qualify as Covered Entities under the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) when handling Protected Health Information (PHI) related to their claimant clients. In those cases, Case Helix acts as a Business Associate.

We enter into Business Associate Agreements (BAAs) with law firm clients as required under HIPAA. Our BAA governs how we handle PHI on behalf of our Covered Entity clients and reflects our obligations with respect to safeguards, breach notification, and permissible uses and disclosures.

The Lead Data we process typically includes general medical condition categories and functional limitation indicators — information that may qualify as PHI in context. We treat all such data with HIPAA-compliant safeguards regardless of whether a BAA has been formally executed.

If you are a law firm and need to execute a BAA before using our services, contact us at Nicholas@casehelix.com.

6. Data Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect the information we hold. These include:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS 1.2 or higher) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel
  • Regular security assessments and vulnerability testing
  • Incident response procedures with breach notification protocols

No method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we use commercially reasonable measures to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

7. Data Retention

We retain Account Data for as long as a client account is active and for a reasonable period thereafter to comply with legal obligations and resolve disputes.

We retain Lead Data for as long as necessary to provide scoring services and maintain audit logs under applicable law. Clients may request deletion of their Lead Data at any time by contacting us, subject to legal retention requirements.

Anonymized and aggregated scoring data — from which no individual can be identified — may be retained indefinitely for model improvement purposes.

8. Claimant Rights

Because Case Helix processes Lead Data on behalf of law firm clients and not directly for claimants, claimants seeking to exercise rights over their personal information (such as access, correction, or deletion) should contact the law firm that collected their information. Case Helix will assist our Clients in responding to such requests as required by law.

Residents of certain states (including California) may have additional rights under applicable privacy laws. Law firms using our platform are responsible for complying with applicable consumer privacy rights with respect to their claimant clients.

9. Cookies and Website Tracking

Our website uses minimal tracking. We may use first-party cookies for basic functionality (such as remembering form state). We do not use third-party advertising cookies or behavioral tracking cookies. You can disable cookies in your browser settings, though this may affect certain website functionality.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will notify clients by email or by posting a prominent notice on our website. The date at the top of this Policy reflects when it was last updated. Continued use of our services after updates constitutes acceptance of the revised Policy.
